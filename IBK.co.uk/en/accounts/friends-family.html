Family Advisor Account Structure


The Family Advisor Account structure is to be used by a natural person "Head of Family" ("HOF") to manage, for free, the accounts of up to five other family members. The Family Advisor Master Account uses a single login to manage a maximum of 15 sub-accounts. The sub-accounts are limited to accounts held by family members or family-held investment vehicles.

  • The master account HOF must be a natural person, may not be registered advisors and may not hold themselves out to the public as advisors.
  • The HOFs may not charge any fees for managing the accounts of other family members.

A HOF can manage the accounts of up to 5 other family members/investment vehicles and a maximum of 15 sub-accounts.

Account Information

The Family Advisor Account structure is distinct from professional advisor account structures that are used by registered investment advisors, investment managers or hedge fund managers. The “Head of Family” (“HOF”) must be a natural person who does not solicit clients or hold himself/herself out to be a financial advisor. Master accounts are provided with administrative functionality to manage up to 15 sub-accounts and cannot receive fees for managing the accounts.
